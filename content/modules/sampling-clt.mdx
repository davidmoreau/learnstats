In this lesson, the population can be normal, uniform, or skewed. Even when the population is skewed, the distribution of sample means becomes more bell-shaped as sample size `n` increases.

<SamplingCLTDemo />

## Why This Matters

When we estimate a population mean from samples, we rely on the sampling distribution. Its spread is the **standard error of the mean (SEM)**, roughly `SD / sqrt(n)`.
